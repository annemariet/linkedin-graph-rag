# Freelance pitch proposals (LUC-100) — with survey-backed numbers

One-line pitches, descriptions, and **quantitative hooks** you can use in outbound, talks, and proposals. Figures below are from **industry surveys** cited in the sources section; replace or extend with any extra percentages from your Linear `survey.md` once merged into this doc.

## Proposal 1: “The AI Pre-Flight Check”

**Pitch:** *I help you kill bad AI bets before they burn six months of calendar and budget.*

**Survey hooks**

- **Use-case and feasibility:** In Gartner’s Q4 2024 survey, **37% of leaders in low-AI-maturity organizations** cited **finding the right use case** among their **top three** implementation barriers — vs. heavier emphasis on security and engineering discipline in higher-maturity orgs ([Gartner press release, June 2025](https://www.gartner.com/en/newsroom/press-releases/2025-06-30-gartner-survey-finds-forty-five-percent-of-organizations-with-high-artificial-intelligence-maturity-keep-artificial-intelligence-projects-operational-for-at-least-three-years)).
- **Data reality check:** **Data availability and quality** ranked among the **top challenges regardless of maturity** — **34%** (low maturity) and **29%** (high maturity) of leaders flagged it (same source).

**Description:** A structured 1–2 week assessment before heavy investment: business metric, data readiness, team readiness, and risk scoring. Deliverable: go/no-go plus a remediation roadmap.

**Main skills:** ML systems thinking, data quality assessment, business–ML translation, risk framing.

**Related problems:** A1 (use-case triage), D1 (pre-project risk).

**Gaps to train:** Formal consulting engagement patterns; sector-specific compliance (health, finance).

**Recurring revenue:** Quarterly portfolio reviews; annual strategy refresh.

**AI agent angle:** Brief-to-risk-scorecard agent; you review before client readout.

## Proposal 2: “The AI Team Doctor”

**Pitch:** *I diagnose the trust, handoffs, and operating-model issues that stall AI — then coach the team to fix them.*

**Survey hooks**

- **Trust and adoption:** **57%** of leaders in **high**-maturity organizations said business units **trust and are ready** to use new AI solutions — vs. **14%** in **low**-maturity organizations (Gartner Q4 2024 survey, n=432; [same press release](https://www.gartner.com/en/newsroom/press-releases/2025-06-30-gartner-survey-finds-forty-five-percent-of-organizations-with-high-artificial-intelligence-maturity-keep-artificial-intelligence-projects-operational-for-at-least-three-years)).
- **Staying power of initiatives:** **45%** of high-maturity orgs kept AI initiatives **in production for three years or more** vs. **20%** in low-maturity orgs — longevity correlates with governance, engineering practices, and value-focused project selection (same source).
- **Leadership and operating model:** **91%** of high-maturity orgs reported **dedicated AI leaders**; **~60%** reported **centralized** AI strategy, governance, data, and infrastructure capabilities (same source).

**Description:** Diagnose silos, unclear ownership, and business–tech tension using validated team-level patterns from your literature stack (e.g. community smells, socio-technical anti-patterns). Follow with coaching, rituals, and light structural recommendations. Monthly retainer friendly.

**Main skills:** Coaching, cross-functional facilitation, ML team dynamics.

**Related problems:** B1 (de-siloing), B2 (manager literacy).

**Gaps to train:** Org psychology basics; formal coaching credentials if you want the label.

**Recurring revenue:** Monthly coaching; quarterly health checks; manager cohorts.

**AI agent angle:** Lightweight periodic surveys mapped to your smell/taxonomy; you interpret.

## Proposal 3: “Notebook to production in 90 days”

**Pitch:** *I turn pilots that impress the board into systems that survive real users, data drift, and on-call.*

**Survey hooks**

- **Production vs. pilot culture:** MIT Sloan Management Review (reporting with BCG) describes organizations running **many pilots and proofs of concept** but **few production deployments**, with much “AI” still legacy ML or small RPA — i.e. a structural **pilot–production gap** rather than a single magic metric ([The Problem With AI Pilots](https://sloanreview.mit.edu/article/the-problem-with-ai-pilots/)).
- **Why discipline matters:** **63%** of high-maturity orgs run **financial risk analysis, ROI analysis, and concrete customer-impact measurement** on AI initiatives — practices associated with sustaining value (Gartner, same press release as above).
- **Security as scale barrier:** **48%** of high-maturity leaders listed **security threats** in their **top three** barriers — a production-hard problem, not a notebook problem (Gartner).

**Description:** For a chosen use case: path to deployment, minimal MLOps, monitoring hooks, ownership handoff. Mix implementation and pairing.

**Main skills:** Pipelines, deployment, experimentation, observability.

**Related problems:** C1 (prototype → production), C2 (tech debt), C3 (MLOps).

**Gaps to train:** Deep platform specifics (Vertex, SageMaker, Databricks); K8s at scale.

**Recurring revenue:** Post-launch maintenance; architecture reviews.

**AI agent angle:** Codebase / artifact scanner for ML tech-debt patterns (literature-backed taxonomy).

## Proposal 4: “The AI Translator”

**Pitch:** *I turn vague stakeholder wishes into ML problem statements, metrics, and adoption plans your engineers can actually build.*

**Survey hooks**

- **Use-case clarity (low maturity):** **37%** of low-maturity leaders — **finding the right use case** in the **top three** barriers (Gartner) — good opener for workshop scope: “from idea to spec.”
- **Metrics culture:** **63%** of high-maturity orgs tie AI work to **ROI and customer impact** measurement (Gartner) — workshops can install a **minimum viable metrics** set for teams that score lower today.

**Description:** Facilitated discovery: success metrics, data needs, workflow change, rollout risks. Part-time, workshop-based.

**Main skills:** Teaching, facilitation, problem framing, stakeholder management.

**Related problems:** A2 (translation), B2 (literacy).

**Gaps to train:** Design / service design methods; vertical domain depth.

**Recurring revenue:** Retainer for new problem framing; licensed training format.

**AI agent angle:** Workshop assistant that turns stakeholder prose into structured briefs; you facilitate.

## Proposal 5: “AI Literacy Masterclass”

**Pitch:** *I teach leaders the few questions that separate demos from durable AI investments.*

**Survey hooks**

- **Trust gap as teaching moment:** **57% vs. 14%** business-unit trust/readiness (high vs. low maturity) — frame the course around **trust, governance, and adoption**, not model trivia (Gartner).
- **Barrier literacy:** Top barriers differ by maturity — **use case** (37% low) vs. **security** (48% high) and **data quality** (~30–34% across maturity) — good module boundaries for executives (Gartner).

**Description:** Short practical sessions: what AI can/can’t do, evaluating proposals, procurement red flags, minimal governance. Feeds inbound for proposals 1–2.

**Main skills:** Public speaking, teaching, synthesis from practice + research.

**Related problems:** B2, A1.

**Gaps to train:** Instructional design; distribution for courses.

**Recurring revenue:** Cohorts, conference keynotes, online course.

**AI agent angle:** Scenario generator for classroom “what broke and why.”

## Recommended starting point (unchanged logic)

- **Package 1 + 2:** bounded assessment → ongoing team coaching (aligns with trust, longevity, and leadership patterns in Gartner data).
- **Add 3** when technical delivery is the bottleneck.
- **Use 5** for **lead generation** and authority; **use the lead-gen question list** in `questions.md` for outbound and first calls.
