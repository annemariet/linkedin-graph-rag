## Gartner — AI maturity & longevity (Q4 2024, published Jun 2025)

**Audience:** 432 leaders (US, UK, France, Germany, India, Japan). Maturity via seven-question Gartner AI Maturity Model (levels 1–5).

| Finding | Figure | Notes |
|--------|--------|--------|
| AI initiatives **in production 3+ years or more** | **45%** high-maturity vs **20%** low-maturity | High maturity ~4.2–4.5 avg.; low ~1.6–2.2 |
| Business units **trust and are ready** to use new AI | **57%** high-maturity vs **14%** low-maturity | Adoption / value step |
| **Data availability & quality** among top AI implementation challenges | **34%** low-maturity, **29%** high-maturity | “Regardless of maturity” theme in release |
| **Finding the right use case** in **top three** barriers | **37%** low-maturity leaders | Contrast with security emphasis in mature orgs |
| **Security threats** in **top three** barriers | **48%** high-maturity leaders | Scale / production-class concerns |
| **Financial risk, ROI, customer-impact** measurement on AI initiatives | **63%** high-maturity | Sustaining value |
| **Dedicated AI leaders** already appointed | **91%** high-maturity | Operating model |
| **Centralized** AI strategy, governance, data, infrastructure | **~60%** (almost 60%) high-maturity | Consistency / efficiency narrative |

**Source:** [Gartner press release, 30 Jun 2025](https://www.gartner.com/en/newsroom/press-releases/2025-06-30-gartner-survey-finds-forty-five-percent-of-organizations-with-high-artificial-intelligence-maturity-keep-artificial-intelligence-projects-operational-for-at-least-three-years)

## Gartner — Pilot → production (Oct–Dec 2021 survey, published Aug 2022)

**Audience:** 699 respondents (US, Germany, UK) at orgs that deployed AI or planned to within three years.

| Finding | Figure | Notes |
|--------|--------|--------|
| AI projects that **make it from pilot to production** (average) | **54%** | Up from **53%** in 2019 Gartner AI in Organizations Survey |
| Orgs applying AI **across several business units** | **One-third** | Strategic vs. tactical AI |
| Execs who **have or can source** AI talent | **72%** | Talent framed as *not* top barrier in this wave |
| Orgs with **thousands of AI models** deployed | **40%** | Governance / ROI demonstration complexity |

**Source:** [Gartner press release, 22 Aug 2022](https://www.gartner.com/en/newsroom/press-releases/2022-08-22-gartner-survey-reveals-80-percent-of-executives-think-automation-can-be-applied-to-any-business-decision)

## Deloitte — State of Generative AI in the Enterprise (Q3 2024 wave)

**Audience:** **2,770** director- to C-suite-level respondents, **14 countries**, May–Jun 2024; all piloting or implementing GenAI.

| Theme | Figure | Notes |
|--------|--------|--------|
| GenAI experiments **fully in production** | **68%** of orgs moved **≤30%** of experiments | Pilot / PoC heavy |
| **Data-related issues** led org to **avoid** certain GenAI use cases | **55%** | Data as gate |
| **Increasing** tech investment in **data management** due to GenAI | **75%** | Foundation spend |
| Top GenAI deployment barriers (**risk-related** in three of top four) | **36%** regulatory compliance; **30%** difficulty managing risks; **29%** lack of governance model | Trust / tightrope narrative |
| Orgs **struggled to define/measure** exact GenAI impacts | **41%** | Measurement gap |
| **Regular CFO reports** on GenAI value | **16%** | Board / finance proof gap |
| Using **specific KPIs** for GenAI performance | **48%** | Partial measurement maturity |
| **54%** seek efficiency/productivity; **38%** **tracking** employee productivity changes | Alignment vs. measurement | |
| Senior exec GenAI interest **high/very high** | **63%** (down **11** pp vs Q1 2024) | Enthusiasm cooling |
| Board interest **high/very high** | **53%** (down **8** pp vs Q1 2024) | |

**Source:** [Deloitte press release — State of Generative AI Q3](https://www2.deloitte.com/us/en/pages/about-deloitte/articles/press-releases/state-of-generative-ai-Q3.html)

## MIT Sloan Management Review — Pilots vs. production (qualitative)

Large organizations: **many** pilots, PoCs, and prototypes; **few** production deployments; much in-production “AI” is **long-standing ML** or **small RPA** — structural **pilot–production gap**, not one headline percentage.

**Source:** [The Problem With AI Pilots](https://sloanreview.mit.edu/article/the-problem-with-ai-pilots/) (Davenport & Bean)
